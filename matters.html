CLASSIFICATION FRAMEWORK

NOIA administers oniric integrity concerns according to operational behavior, not origin, intent, or appearance.

Matters are categorized into two primary domains: Subjects and Events. Each domain is evaluated using distinct classification scales.

SUBJECTS

Discrete individuals capable of agency. Subjects are assessed based on their capacity to act, traverse, persist, and compromise oniric continuity.

  • Agency Level (AG)
  • Transversal Capability (TRV)
  • Host Dependency (HST)
  • Oniric Footprint Persistence (OFP)
  • Containment Feasibility (CNMT)
  • Integrity Threat Index (ITI)

EVENTS

Systemic phenomena lacking individual intent. Events are assessed based on recurrence, spread, and structural impact on oniric environments.

  • Recurrence Rate
  • Propagation Scope
  • Causal Depth
  • Containment Difficulty
